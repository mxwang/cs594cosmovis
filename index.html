Project Proposal

I. Introduction:

- Describe the background of the cosmology application for the contest.

During its 13.7 billion year of our universe, it progressed from small fluctuations that we see in the cosmic microwave background to today, where we are surrounded by galaxies and clusters of galaxies interconnected by a vast cosmic web. The simulations of the formation of structure in the Universe would benefit our understanding of the universe. However, it is very challenge to simulate this large number of particles, and spatial structure forming by gravitational interaction and collapse. Typically, the halo finding plays the key role in the structure identification. How the structures of halo and the substructures inside halo interact, merge, separate and grow is our interest for the cosmology simulation.

- Overview the specific tasks listed in the contest that you will be tackling.

Mainly, the test is to create a basic framework to ingest, process, organize and visualize the particle data. Specifically, the framework should be able to process the high-dimensionality datasets of halo, and visualize the properties of halos(position, velocity, angular momentum, etc). Moreover, the semi-analytical models would be provided in the simulation to analyze the structure formation. Furthermore, diving deep into the Halo substructure, such as coloring substructure components by mass with varying shades of color shared across all substructure components, that would help us getting a deep sense of how star formed, etc.

- Define the scope of your project and the expected results.

In our project, the main task is to identify and visualize the halos and their evolution. First, we would create a framework to ingest, process, organize and visualize the data. Second, in the stage of halos identification, halos and the substructures of halos would be identified, and output the file which saved the total numbers and their characteristic. Third, some visualization of the halos evolution would be designed. We expect to have 3-D image of the dark space constituting of the halos, and the substructure of the halo could be observed after zooming in the image. Moreover, Tracking of the halos would be implemented using the data of different time, and the halos merge tree would be obtained based on the tracking results.

II. The data:

- Describe the contest datasets: the formats, the information contained in the data sets and the relationships between the data sets.

The data format is SDF.This format is composed of a human readable ASCII header followed by raw binary data. It provides the raw particle data, which is described by a position vector, velocity vector, and unique particle identifier. The data: starting from ds14_scivis_0128_e4_dt04_0.0200 to ds14_scivis_0128_e4_dt04_1.0000, are the snapshots of different time.

III. The tasks:

- Explain the selected tasks in more detail

Task1: 1. The user could choose which data to load 2.After loading the data, the user could analyze the data. For instance, finding the halos or the substructures of the halos. 3. The data could be well organized. The data of different time would be compared to obtaint the halos evolution information. 4. The user would have many choices of the visualization, and they can choose the one to show the information they want.

Task2: Step1: halo identification: based on the special mass, density character of halos, they can be identified. And output the file which stores the halos information row by row, and the columns includes: mass, position, and velocity and so on. Step 2: Obtain the Merger trees which describe the evolution of halos over time. With mapping the halos properties, we track the merging or spliting apart of those halos. Another difficulty is how to well illustrate of the evolution course by the visualization techniques.

Task4:Step 1: halo substructure identification: inside a halo, also use the mass, density character to recognize the halo substrures Step 2: Visualize the halo substrures: For instance, in a 3D image, use differe colors to indicate the different properties. Besides, we can also visualize the substrucure evolution, such as their transfering from one host halo to another.
